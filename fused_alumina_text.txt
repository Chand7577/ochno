Section 4 — Brown Fused Alumina (BFA) — full specifications
Production: fusing calcined bauxite with anthracite (carbon) and iron scrap in an electric arc furnace at ~2000–2200°C. TiO₂ from bauxite remains in finished grain as a toughening agent — this is what gives BFA its characteristic brown colour and impact resistance.

Section 5 — WFA vs BFA — which grade do you need?
Plain-language selection guide for Indian buyers:
Grinding stainless steel, titanium, aluminium, copper or non-ferrous metals? → WFA only — iron from BFA contaminates the work surface and causes corrosion
Grinding high-speed steel, alloy steel, hardened tool steel? → WFA preferred — self-sharpening, cool cutting, prevents workpiece burn
General-purpose grinding of carbon steel, cast iron, low-alloy steel? → BFA (regular grade) — more cost-effective, tough grain survives heavy feed rates
Blasting carbon or structural steel for rust removal / surface preparation? → BFA F16–F46 standard; low-iron BFA for aluminium / copper profiles
Making vitrified grinding wheels for precision applications? → WFA (friable, cool cutting) or high-purity BFA (tougher, for carbon steel)
High-temperature refractories (above 1600°C), castables, kiln furniture? → WFA refractory grade (higher purity, better slag resistance)
Polishing, lapping, semiconductor or optical glass? → WFA micro-powder F280–F1200
Not sure? Share your material, application and surface finish requirement — we confirm the right grade and grit size
