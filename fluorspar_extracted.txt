4.  Who in India buys fluorspar and why
Fluorspar is used across 8+ Indian industries
HF / AHF manufacturing (largest segment): SRF, Navin Fluorine, Gujarat Fluorochemicals — require 97%+ acidspar continuously; HF is the entry point for the entire fluorochemical chain
Refrigerants & fluoropolymers: PTFE, PVDF, FKM manufacturers — India's fluoropolymer sector is growing rapidly post China+1 trend; needs consistent acidspar supply
Steel & alloy industry: metspar as flux in EAF, BOF; lowers slag viscosity, improves desulphurisation — Tata Steel, JSW, SAIL type buyers; 85–92% CaF₂ sufficient
Aluminium smelting: flux in electrolytic cells (Hall-Héroult process), reduces bath temperature
Glass & ceramics: opacity agent, flux to lower melting point — architectural glass, opalescent glass, ceramic frit
Cement industry: mineraliser in clinker formation, reduces kiln temperature, improves grindability
Welding electrodes: flux coating component — mid-purity grade sufficient
Optical & semiconductor: UV-grade CaF₂ for lens blanks, lithography windows — niche but high-value

7. FAQ
1. What is the difference between acid grade and metallurgical grade fluorspar?
Acid grade fluorspar typically contains ≥97% CaF₂ and is used in hydrofluoric acid and chemical production.Metallurgical grade fluorspar contains 80–90% CaF₂ and is primarily used in steelmaking as a flux to improve slag fluidity.
