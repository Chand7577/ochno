Section 2 — What are industrial fatty acids?
Fatty acids are long-chain carboxylic acids derived from natural fats and oils — plant-based (palm, rice bran, tall oil) or produced as industrial by-products. They are among the most versatile raw materials in industrial chemistry, serving as the base for soaps, detergents, lubricants, paints, rubber, cosmetics, animal feed and biodiesel.

India is a large consumer of industrial fatty acids — the soap and detergent manufacturing sector, the paints and alkyd resin industry, the rubber and tyre sector, and the oleochemical industry together consume hundreds of thousands of tonnes annually. Specialty and higher-purity grades are imported primarily from Malaysia, Indonesia, Russia, Ukraine, Finland and the USA.

We supply five key product categories — PFAD, TOFA, Stearic Acid, Oleic Acid and Rice Bran Fatty Acid — each explained below with full specifications.

Section 3 — PFAD (Palm Fatty Acid Distillate)
What is PFAD?
PFAD is a processing residue from the physical refining of crude palm oil. At room temperature it is a light brown semi-solid that melts to a brown liquid when heated. It contains 70–80% free fatty acids — primarily palmitic and oleic acids — with the remaining 20% comprising triglycerides, partial glycerides, vitamin E, sterols and squalene.

It is one of the most cost-effective fatty acid raw materials available — significantly cheaper than refined palm oil or individual fatty acid fractions — making it the preferred choice for price-sensitive soap, biodiesel and animal feed applications.

Source countries: Malaysia and Indonesia — both have the world's largest palm oil refining industries generating PFAD as a consistent by-product. These two countries together supply over 85% of India's PFAD imports.

Section 4 — TOFA (Tall Oil Fatty Acid)
What is TOFA?
Tall Oil Fatty Acid (TOFA) is a low-cost unsaturated fatty acid sourced from crude tall oil — the by-product of the kraft process of paper manufacture. It is a viscous, yellow-black liquid containing mostly oleic and linoleic acids with a carboxyl group and long carbon chain that gives it excellent esterification and emulsifying properties.

India has limited domestic production of pine derivatives and relies entirely on imports for TOFA. Primary sources are Russia, Ukraine, Finland, Sweden and USA — all countries with large kraft pulp and paper industries. Russia and Ukraine are among the most price-competitive sources; Finland (Forchem) and Sweden produce premium low-rosin grades preferred for alkyd resins and dimer acids.

Section 5 — Stearic Acid
What is stearic acid?
Stearic acid (C18:0) is a saturated fatty acid derived from palm stearin or animal tallow. It is a white waxy solid — one of the most widely used fatty acids across rubber, plastics, cosmetics and personal care. India is both a producer and importer of stearic acid — triple pressed high-purity grades are often imported from Malaysia.

Section 5A — Rice Bran Fatty Acid (RBFA) — Exported from India
What is Rice Bran Fatty Acid?
Rice Bran Fatty Acid (RBFA) is a by-product recovered during the refining of crude rice bran oil — one of India's most abundant vegetable oil by-products. Unlike PFAD and TOFA which are imported, India is a net exporter of RBFA — generated in large quantities from rice bran oil refineries in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh and Punjab.

RBFA is naturally rich in tocopherols (Vitamin E) and oryzanol with a balanced C16–C18 fatty acid profile, giving it functional advantages beyond ordinary fatty acids in personal care and cosmetic applications.

Frequently Asked Questions
Q1. What is PFAD and why is it used in soap manufacturing?
PFAD contains 70–90% free fatty acids — primarily palmitic and oleic — which directly saponify with caustic soda to produce soap. It is the most cost-effective fatty acid feedstock for laundry soap and industrial soap production, significantly cheaper than refined palm oil.

Q2. What is the difference between TOFA Grade 1 and TOFA Grade 3?
Grade 1 has ≤5% rosin acid and is used in premium applications like alkyd resins, dimer acids and lubricant esters — sourced primarily from Finland and Sweden. Grade 3 has 20–25% rosin acid and is used in drilling fluids and construction chemicals — typically sourced from Russia and Ukraine at more competitive pricing.

Q3. What is the difference between single, double and triple pressed stearic acid?
The pressing grades differ in C18:0 purity — single pressed (~45%) suits rubber and candles; double pressed (~90%) is standard for plastics and PVC; triple pressed (≥95%) is for cosmetics and pharmaceuticals requiring very high purity and low iodine value.

Q4. Can PFAD be used for biodiesel production?
Yes — PFAD's high FFA content makes it ideal for acid-catalysed esterification into biodiesel (FAME). It is a low-cost feedstock for B5–B20 biodiesel blends; the FFA must be esterified before transesterification to avoid soap formation.

Q5. What is Rice Bran Fatty Acid and how is it different from PFAD?
RBFA comes from rice bran oil refining — an India-origin by-product rich in tocopherols and oryzanol, with a C16–C18 fatty acid profile similar to PFAD but with natural Vitamin E content. Unlike PFAD which India imports, RBFA is exported from India to Bangladesh, Vietnam and Gulf markets.

Q6. What is the MOQ and sample lead time?
MOQ is 1 MT for drum-packed grades (stearic, TOFA) and 20 MT for flexibag PFAD. Samples with full COA are dispatched within 3–5 days. Contact us with your product, grade and application and we will confirm pricing and availability.
