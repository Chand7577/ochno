Why Indian wollastonite leads
India holds ~40% of global wollastonite reserves — this section positions you as a knowledgeable insider who understands the mineral, not just a trading intermediary
Rajasthan (Sirohi, Dungarpur, Pali): largest reserves; high-purity CaSiO₃, naturally bright white, low Fe₂O₃ — ideal for plastics and paints applications where colour is critical
Gujarat (Chhota Udaipur, Bharuch): good quality deposits; closer to Mundra port — logistics advantage for export
Geology note: wollastonite forms at the contact zone between limestone and silica-rich intrusions (skarn deposits) under high temperature and pressure — India's Precambrian terrain is ideal, producing naturally acicular (needle-shaped) crystals with high aspect ratios
Processing chain: open-pit mining → jaw crushing → rod milling / impact milling (for HAR preservation) → air classification → surface treatment (if required) → packaging
Key note: HAR grades require special milling — impact mills preserve needle length better than ball mills; mention if you use dedicated HAR processing line

Industry applications deep-dive
Wollastonite's acicular morphology gives it functional properties no other white filler can replicate
Ceramics & tiles (largest market): flux in ceramic body — reduces firing temperature by 50–100°C (energy saving), controls shrinkage, improves green strength; ideal for fast-fire wall tiles; also used in sanitaryware, technical ceramics and glazes; mention thermal expansion coefficient compatibility
Plastics & polymer compounds: HAR grade reinforces nylon (PA6/PA66), polypropylene, ABS — improves flexural modulus, reduces warpage, enhances dimensional stability; replaces or supplements glass fibre at lower cost and lower abrasion to processing equipment; surface-treated grade improves matrix adhesion
Paints & coatings: functional extender replacing TiO₂ partially — improves scrub resistance, reduces gloss where needed, flattens sheen; in anti-corrosion coatings acts as barrier pigment due to plate-like/needle orientation; reduces cracking in exterior paints
Friction materials & brake pads: replaces asbestos as reinforcing fibre — improves thermal stability, reduces brake fade, meets environmental regulations; major growth segment as global asbestos phase-out continues
Metallurgy (steel / iron foundry): lump/crushed grade as flux — lowers slag melting point, improves slag fluidity, acts as slag conditioner in EAF / BOF; also used in welding flux formulations
Sealants, adhesives & construction: improves thixotropy in sealants, reduces slump; in construction boards as filler for fibre cement panels
Agriculture: slowly releases calcium and silicon — used as soil amendment in silicon-deficient soils; growing niche in India's own agricultural market
