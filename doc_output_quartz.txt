Quartz &amp; Quartzite 
1. Quartz in India (States &amp; Grades)
Andhra Pradesh / Telangana → High purity quartz (99–99.9% SiO₂) – export &amp; glass grade – Limited availability
Tamil Nadu / Karnataka → Industrial quartz – ceramic, ferro alloy use
Rajasthan / Gujarat → Quartz &amp; quartzite – steel, ramming mass
Odisha / Northeast → Medium grade – foundry &amp; construction
Forms: lumps, grits, powder, silica sand
2. Quartz vs Quartzite, In which industry what is used and why 
Quartz → Pure mineral, high chemical purity  sio2 is  96 to 99.9%
Quartzite → Metamorphic rock, stronger &amp; heat-resistant sio2 is  94 to 96%
Quartz → used in glass/electronics
Quartzite → used in steel &amp; refractory
3. Industry Usage by Grade
Steel / Ferro Alloys → Quartzite lumps (95–98% SiO₂)
Refractory → Quartzite for furnace lining &amp; ramming mass
Glass Industry → High purity quartz (99%+ SiO₂, low Fe)
Foundry → Silica sand / quartz grains
Electronics / Solar → Ultra high purity quartz (&gt;99.9%)
